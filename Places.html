Paris

The capital of France, Paris, is one of the world's major centers of culture, art, fashion and gastronomy. In the central part of the city, built in the 19th century, the River Seine crosses near wide boulevards. The most outstanding sights of Paris are the Eiffel Tower and Notre Dame Cathedral in the Gothic style, approaching in the XII approach.

Los Angeles

Los Angeles is a huge city in southern California, the capital of the American film and television industry. The Hollywood area, whose name is laid out on a hill in huge letters, has gained worldwide fame thanks to the film studios Paramount Pictures, Universal and Warner Brothers.

New York City

New York includes five boroughs (districts) located at the confluence of the Hudson River into the Atlantic Ocean. In the city center is densely populated Manhattan - one of the world's largest commercial, financial and cultural centers. The main attractions of New York are numerous skyscrapers, including the Empire State Building, and huge Central Park.
